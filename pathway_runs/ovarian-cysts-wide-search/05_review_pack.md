# DGRefHelp Review Pack: Ovarian cysts (wide-search)

## 1) Scope
Primary-care triage and referral thresholds for adult ovarian cysts/masses, including suspected malignancy escalation.

## 2) Tiered search summary
- **Tier 1 completed:** NICE, NICE CKS (access check), SIGN, Scottish Referral Guidelines (NHS Scotland), Right Decisions, territorial boards (NHS GGC, NHS Borders, NHS Lothian RefHelp).
- **Tier 2 completed:** RCOG Green-top guidance (No. 34, No. 62).
- **Tier 3:** not required (evidence saturation reached with concordant thresholds).

## 3) Key threshold outputs (concise)

### Immediate same-day emergency
- Suspected ovarian torsion/rupture/haemorrhage with acute severe pain -> same-day emergency gynaecology assessment.

### USC / suspected cancer triggers
- Ascites or palpable pelvic/abdominal mass not clearly uterine fibroid/GI/urological.
- CA125 **>=35 IU/ml** with suspicious symptom profile.
- Ultrasound suspicious for ovarian malignancy.

### Premenopausal
- Simple cyst **<5 cm**: usually no routine follow-up.
- Simple cyst **5-7 cm**: interval TVUS (commonly 6-monthly up to 1 year).
- Simple cyst **>7 cm**: gynaecology referral.
- Complex/non-simple mass: CA125 (+ LDH/AFP/hCG if <40) and risk-based referral.

### Postmenopausal
- Simple unilateral unilocular cyst **<=3 cm**: no routine follow-up (aligned with RCOG update and current local pathways).
- **3-5 cm** with CA125 <35: TVUS + CA125 surveillance (6-monthly up to 1 year).
- **>5 cm**: urgent referral.
- Any non-simple/indeterminate mass: refer with CA125 and RMI.

### RMI note
- Local Scottish pathways commonly use **RMI >200** for high-risk escalation.
- NICE secondary-care recommendation cites **RMI >=250** for specialist MDT referral.
- Practical pathway stance: escalate if local high-risk threshold reached.

## 4) Conflicts / caveats
- **RMI threshold variance** (200 local operational vs 250 NICE specialist threshold).
- NHS Borders page is useful but older review date; prioritised newer GGC and Scottish national referral criteria where discordant.
- CKS page is access-limited in extraction environment.
- PDF extraction for some specialty PDFs was technically limited; thresholds corroborated across indexed summaries and local pathways.

## 5) Evidence Confidence
**Moderate-High.**
Rationale: consistent threshold agreement across NICE, Scottish national referral guidance, Right Decisions territorial pathways, and RCOG specialty standards; minor threshold variance and extraction/access limitations do not materially alter practical referral decisions.
