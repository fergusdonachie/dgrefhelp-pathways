# Fatty liver (MASLD) in adults — DGRefHelp review draft (v1)

## Assessment
- Confirm likely MASLD in adults with steatosis risk factors (obesity, T2DM, dyslipidaemia, hypertension, metabolic syndrome) and/or persistent LFT abnormality.
- Exclude alternative liver disease causes where indicated (alcohol-related liver disease, viral hepatitis, autoimmune/cholestatic disease, drug-induced injury, haemochromatosis where clinically suggested).
- Record alcohol intake, weight/BMI (or waist), BP, HbA1c, lipid profile, LFTs, FBC and platelet count.
- Calculate fibrosis risk using **FIB-4** and **LiverRisk**.
- Use age-adjusted FIB-4 referral thresholds:
  - Age <65 years: refer if **FIB-4 >1.3**
  - Age ≥65 years: refer if **FIB-4 >2.0**
- Refer if **LiverRisk score >10**.

## Primary care management
- Explain diagnosis and risk: MASLD is primarily cardiometabolic disease with liver consequence.
- Weight management: set a structured target (typically 7–10% weight loss where feasible) and support with diet/activity plan.
- Alcohol: advise minimisation/abstinence when liver injury is present.
- Optimise diabetes, blood pressure and lipids; treat cardiovascular risk aggressively.
- **Statins should not be routinely withheld** solely due to mild stable transaminitis in fatty liver.
- Consider evidence-based anti-obesity/diabetes pharmacotherapy (including GLP-1 receptor agonist pathways where clinically indicated) according to local formulary and comorbidity profile.
- Monitor in primary care when below referral threshold and clinically stable:
  - Repeat LFTs/metabolic markers and recalculate risk periodically (typically 1–3 yearly by risk profile).

## Who to refer
### Hospital admission (same day)
- Suspected decompensated chronic liver disease: jaundice with synthetic failure, ascites, encephalopathy, variceal bleeding, sepsis with liver failure concern.

### Urgent suspicion of cancer (USOC)
- Imaging or examination suspicious of hepatocellular carcinoma or other hepatobiliary malignancy.

### Urgent (non-cancer outpatient)
- Significant or rapidly worsening liver biochemistry with concern for advanced chronic liver disease.
- Probable chronic liver disease with systemic features requiring prompt specialist assessment.

### Routine (outpatient)
- **FIB-4 >1.3** (age <65) or **FIB-4 >2.0** (age ≥65).
- **LiverRisk score >10**.
- Diagnostic uncertainty, suspected alternative chronic liver disease, or persistent unexplained abnormalities despite first-line workup.

### Include in referral
- Duration/pattern of LFT abnormality; alcohol history.
- BMI/weight trajectory and cardiometabolic comorbidities.
- FIB-4 value, LiverRisk value, and dates of blood tests used.
- Relevant ultrasound/imaging reports.
- Medication list and interventions already started.

## Who not to refer
- Adults with likely MASLD who are clinically stable and **below all referral thresholds** (FIB-4 and LiverRisk), with no red-flag features.
- Continue active primary care management and periodic reassessment.
