Ovarian cysts are common and most are benign. In premenopausal women, malignancy is uncommon; risk rises after menopause. The key decision is identifying the smaller group with acute complications or cancer risk.

Patients often present with bloating, pelvic pain, urinary symptoms, or are found incidentally on ultrasound. Use menopausal status, ultrasound morphology/size, and (in postmenopausal women) CA125 with RMI (Risk of Malignancy Index) to decide who can be managed conservatively and who needs referral.
