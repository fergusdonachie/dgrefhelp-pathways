Most ovarian cysts are benign. The key decision is identifying the smaller group with acute complications or malignancy risk.

Patients often present with bloating, pelvic pain, urinary symptoms, or are found incidentally on ultrasound. Use menopausal status, ultrasound morphology/size, and (in postmenopausal women) CA125 with RMI to decide who can be managed conservatively and who needs referral.
