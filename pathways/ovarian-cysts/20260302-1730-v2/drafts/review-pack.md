# Ovarian cysts in adults

## Page contents (Background)
Most ovarian cysts seen in primary care are benign, but the key safety task is to identify the smaller group with acute complications (for example torsion/rupture) or malignancy risk.

Patients may present with persistent abdominal distension/fullness, pelvic or lower abdominal pain, early satiety, urinary urgency/frequency, or acute severe unilateral pain. Some cysts are found incidentally on ultrasound requested for other symptoms.

The pathway prioritises rapid recognition of emergency and cancer-risk features, while avoiding over-investigation of clearly low-risk simple cysts.

## Accordion: Assessment
- Confirm menopausal status (major risk stratifier).
- Take focused history:
  - pain pattern (including acute severe onset suggesting torsion/rupture),
  - bloating/fullness, appetite change, urinary symptoms,
  - family history of ovarian/breast/colorectal cancer,
  - prior cyst history and interval change.
- Examine:
  - abdominal examination for mass, tenderness, guarding/peritonism, distension,
  - pelvic/bimanual examination where appropriate,
  - assess for clinical ascites/systemic concern.
- Blood tests:
  - Postmenopausal: CA125 for any ovarian mass.
  - Premenopausal: CA125 only for complex/suspicious masses (not routine for clearly simple cysts); consider LDH/AFP/hCG in relevant complex masses where germ-cell pathology is a concern.
- Imaging:
  - First line: transvaginal USS (or transabdominal if TVUSS not possible).
  - Classify cyst morphology and size (simple/unilocular vs complex; unilateral/bilateral; solid components/septations/ascites).
- Postmenopausal risk score:
  - calculate RMI = U x M x CA125.

## Accordion: Primary care management
- If emergency features (acute severe pain, peritonism, suspected torsion/rupture, haemodynamic concern): same-day hospital assessment.
- Premenopausal simple cysts:
  - <5 cm: reassure, no routine follow-up.
  - 5-7 cm: arrange interval USS follow-up (typically 3-6 months).
  - >7 cm, persistent, or enlarging: refer to gynaecology.
- Postmenopausal simple unilateral unilocular cysts:
  - <=3 cm: low risk, no routine follow-up.
  - >3 to <5 cm with normal CA125 and no suspicious features: conservative management with interval USS follow-up.
  - >5 cm, persistent, symptomatic, rising CA125, or suspicious features: refer.
- Complex cysts in any group: treat as higher risk and refer according to urgency criteria.
- Provide explicit safety-net advice on red flags (worsening pain, vomiting, increasing distension, persistent symptoms, systemic deterioration).

## Accordion: Who to refer
### Hospital admission (same day)
- Suspected ovarian torsion.
- Suspected ruptured/haemorrhagic cyst with acute abdomen.
- Haemodynamic instability, sepsis concern, or uncontrolled acute pain.

### Urgent suspicion of cancer (USOC)
- Postmenopausal RMI >200.
- Suspicious malignant USS features (for example solid areas/papillary projections, ascites, bilateral complex mass, metastatic features).
- Clinical picture strongly suspicious for ovarian malignancy.

### Urgent (non-cancer outpatient)
- Significant symptomatic complex cyst where malignancy pathway criteria are not yet met.
- Irreducible diagnostic uncertainty with ongoing concerning symptoms requiring rapid specialist review.

### Routine (outpatient)
- Premenopausal simple cyst >7 cm, or persistent/enlarging cyst despite interval follow-up.
- Postmenopausal cyst >5 cm or persistent cyst requiring specialist planning.
- Persistent symptoms with indeterminate but non-USOC imaging profile.

### Include in referral
- Menopausal status and symptom duration/progression.
- USS morphology and size, including bilaterality and ascites/solid features.
- CA125 and RMI (postmenopausal), plus relevant additional markers if performed.
- Family history risk factors, comorbidities, and prior imaging chronology.

## Accordion: Who not to refer
- Premenopausal simple cysts <5 cm in otherwise well patients.
- Postmenopausal simple unilateral unilocular cysts <=3 cm with no concerning features.
- Selected postmenopausal >3 to <5 cm simple cysts with normal CA125 and stable interval imaging.
