Most ovarian cysts are benign, but primary care must identify those linked to acute illness or malignancy risk.

Typical presentations include persistent abdominal distension/fullness, pelvic pain, early satiety, urinary frequency/urgency, or acute severe unilateral pain. Some cysts are incidental ultrasound findings.

Management should be risk-stratified by menopausal status, ultrasound morphology/size, and CA125/RMI where indicated.
