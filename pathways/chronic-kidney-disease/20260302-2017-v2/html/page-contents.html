CKD is common and usually managed in primary care. Most patients are picked up on routine eGFR and urine ACR testing before symptoms develop.

Use a simple approach: confirm persistence for at least 3 months, stage using eGFR (G-stage) and ACR (A-stage), reduce cardiovascular and renal risk, and refer the higher-risk group using clear thresholds (including KFRE in CKD G3a-G5).
