Key management principle: Exclude central and emergency causes first. If red flags are absent and a peripheral diagnosis is likely, treat in primary care and refer only when symptoms persist or risk profile is higher.

  • Use focused neurological assessment and symptom-pattern classification.
  • Treat confirmed BPPV promptly with repositioning manoeuvres.
  • Escalate immediately for focal neurology, severe ataxia, reduced consciousness or other central red flags.
