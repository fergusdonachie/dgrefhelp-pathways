Frailty in adults: primary care identification, management and referral

Identify frailty proactively, reduce avoidable harm from treatment burden and minor stressors, and coordinate person-centred care around function, goals, and safety.

  • Assessment: use practical frailty tools and clinical judgement; assess function, falls risk, medicines burden, and support needs.
  • Primary care management: agree an individualised plan, optimise medicines, prevent falls, and review nutrition/hydration/cognition.
  • Escalation: urgent referral for acute deterioration or unsafe home context; routine specialist referral for recurrent falls/progressive decline/complex polypharmacy.
