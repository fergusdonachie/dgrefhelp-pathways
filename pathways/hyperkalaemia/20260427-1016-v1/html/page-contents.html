Hyperkalaemia in adults (primary care): assessment, management and referral

Confirm the result, exclude pseudohyperkalaemia, identify reversible drivers and decide between community management or hospital admission based on severity, ECG and acute illness / AKI status.

  • Immediate admission (999 / ED): any K⁺ ≥6.5 mmol/L; any K⁺ ≥6.0 with ECG changes; mild–moderate hyperkalaemia in an acutely ill patient or with AKI.
  • Community management: mild (5.5–5.9) and moderate (6.0–6.4) when clinically well, no AKI — review medication, diet, treat acidosis, monitor.
  • K⁺ binders (Patiromer / SZC): secondary-care-initiated only, for CKD 3b–5 (not on dialysis) or HF with persistent K⁺ ≥6.0 on sub-optimal RAASi.
  • Repeat testing intervals follow Renal Association / Think Kidneys Table 4.
