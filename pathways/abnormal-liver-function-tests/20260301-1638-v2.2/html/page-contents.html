Abnormal liver function tests (LFTs) in adults: stepwise primary care pathway

Use a structured sequence to reduce uncertainty: identify urgent risk first, define the biochemical pattern, complete focused first-line tests, then decide follow-up versus referral.

  • Step 1 (same-day safety): check for jaundice with synthetic dysfunction, ascites, encephalopathy, bleeding, severe systemic illness, or rapidly worsening bloods.
  • Step 2 (pattern): classify hepatocellular (ALT/AST), cholestatic (ALP/GGT), isolated bilirubin, isolated GGT, or mixed pattern.
  • Step 3 (cause + severity): perform focused liver screen, ultrasound where indicated, and fibrosis risk triage in likely steatotic/alcohol-related disease.
  • Step 4 (action): manage low-risk stable patterns in primary care with timed review; escalate persistent, progressive, cholestatic, or synthetic-failure patterns.
