Abnormal liver function tests (LFTs) in adults: practical primary care interpretation and referral

Liver blood tests include enzymes (ALT, AST, ALP, GGT), bilirubin, and markers of liver synthetic function (albumin and INR/PT). In primary care, interpretation should combine pattern, severity, and trend with clinical context rather than relying on a single isolated value.

  • In D&G, routine LFT is limited: ALT, ALP, bilirubin. Request AST, GGT, albumin, FBC (platelets), and INR/PT separately when assessing abnormalities.
  • Enzymes suggest injury pattern: ALT/AST usually indicate hepatocellular injury; ALP with GGT suggests cholestatic/biliary pattern; isolated ALP may be non-hepatic.
  • Synthetic function reflects liver performance: low albumin and/or raised INR/PT can indicate impaired liver function and carry higher urgency than mild isolated enzyme derangement.
  • Platelets matter: low or falling platelets with abnormal liver tests may indicate portal hypertension/advanced fibrosis and should lower referral threshold.
