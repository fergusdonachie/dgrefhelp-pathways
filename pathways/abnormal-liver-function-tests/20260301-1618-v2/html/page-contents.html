Abnormal liver function tests (LFTs) in adults: practical primary care assessment and referral

Use a pattern-based approach: identify urgent red flags first, classify the dominant abnormality, investigate persistent abnormalities systematically, and stage fibrosis risk in likely chronic liver disease.

  • Immediate escalation: synthetic failure/decompensation signs, severe cholestatic concern, or rapidly worsening derangement.
  • Pattern-led management: hepatocellular, cholestatic, isolated GGT, isolated bilirubin, and synthetic-marker abnormalities are handled differently.
  • Primary care objective: identify treatable causes early and avoid both delayed referral in high-risk cases and unnecessary referral in low-risk stable cases.
