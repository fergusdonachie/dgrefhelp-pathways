Abnormal liver function tests (LFTs) in adults: practical primary care interpretation and referral

Liver blood tests include enzymes (ALT, AST, ALP, GGT), bilirubin, and markers of liver synthetic function (albumin and INR/PT). In primary care, interpretation should combine pattern, severity, and trend with clinical context rather than relying on a single isolated value.

  • Enzymes suggest injury pattern: ALT/AST usually indicate hepatocellular injury; ALP with GGT suggests cholestatic/biliary pattern; isolated ALP may be non-hepatic.
  • Bilirubin helps define cholestatic vs unconjugated patterns: isolated unconjugated rise may be benign (for example Gilbert context), but jaundice with other abnormalities needs urgent assessment.
  • Synthetic function reflects liver performance, not just injury: low albumin and/or raised INR/PT can indicate impaired liver function and carry higher urgency than mild isolated enzyme derangement.
  • Concerning features: synthetic dysfunction, decompensation features, progressive cholestatic pattern, severe or rising abnormalities, or persistent unexplained derangement.
