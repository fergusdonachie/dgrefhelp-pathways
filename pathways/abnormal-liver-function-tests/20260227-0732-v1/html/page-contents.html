Abnormal liver function tests in adults: primary care triage and referral

Interpret abnormal liver blood tests in clinical context: symptoms/signs, risk factors, biochemical pattern, and trend over time.

  • Urgent same-day referral if jaundice with synthetic dysfunction (raised INR/prolonged PT, low albumin), ascites, encephalopathy, bleeding, marked cholestasis with weight loss, or marked biochemical derangement.
  • Mild abnormalities (<2× ULN): repeat tests, review alcohol/medicines, assess risk factors, and safety-net.
  • Significant abnormalities (>2–3× ULN): arrange further investigation and consider early specialist referral.
  • Persistent unexplained abnormality: refer to secondary care/hepatology.

Differentiate hepatocellular and cholestatic patterns, and treat synthetic dysfunction as a high-risk finding irrespective of enzyme magnitude.
