# Unscheduled bleeding on HRT — DGRefHelp review draft (v1)

## Assessment
- Confirm HRT type, route, dose and timing:
  - Continuous combined HRT (ccHRT) vs sequential HRT (sHRT)
  - Date started, and date of any recent change.
- Characterise bleeding pattern: onset timing, heaviness, duration, frequency, intercurrent pelvic symptoms.
- Check endometrial cancer risk factors and co-factors (e.g. obesity, prolonged unopposed estrogen exposure, hereditary risk context).
- Exclude non-endometrial bleeding sources where indicated (cervical/vaginal pathology, infection, trauma).
- Review progestogen adequacy and adherence in women with a uterus.

## Primary care management
- For low-risk women, if unscheduled bleeding occurs:
  - within 6 months of starting HRT, or
  - persists up to 3 months after changing HRT dose/preparation,
  offer HRT/progestogen optimisation for a total of up to 6 months.
- Optimise regimen before investigation where safe:
  - ensure adequate monthly progestogen exposure,
  - address adherence and regimen errors,
  - consider alternative progestogen strategy/preparation if needed.
- Safety-net throughout: earlier escalation for heavy/prolonged or worsening bleeding.

## Who to refer
### Hospital admission (same day)
- Haemodynamic instability, severe acute blood loss, or other acute emergency features.

### Urgent suspicion of cancer (USOC)
- Significant concern for endometrial malignancy based on bleeding pattern/risk profile/imaging.
- Thickened endometrium above regimen-specific reassurance thresholds on TVUS with concerning clinical context.

### Urgent (non-cancer outpatient)
- Urgent transvaginal ultrasound (within 6 weeks) if:
  - first presentation of unscheduled bleeding is >6 months after HRT initiation, or
  - bleeding persists >3 months after HRT dose/preparation change, or
  - bleeding is heavy/prolonged, or
  - additional endometrial-cancer risk factors are present.

### Routine (outpatient)
- Ongoing unscheduled bleeding despite 6 months of structured HRT/progestogen adjustments in otherwise low-risk women.
- Diagnostic uncertainty requiring specialist review.

### Include in referral
- HRT regimen details (ccHRT/sHRT, doses, route, start/change dates).
- Full bleeding chronology and severity.
- Relevant risk factors and examination findings.
- TVUS findings (including endometrial thickness) and any prior investigations.
- Interventions already attempted and response.

## Who not to refer
- Low-risk unscheduled bleeding occurring within the expected early-adjustment window and improving with correct regimen optimisation.
