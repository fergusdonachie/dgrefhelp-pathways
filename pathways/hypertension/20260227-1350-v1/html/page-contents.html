Hypertension in adults (primary care): diagnosis, treatment and referral

Confirm diagnosis using ABPM or HBPM, assess cardiovascular risk and target organ damage, then treat using a structured stepwise regimen with active safety monitoring.

  • Urgent same-day referral: BP >=180/120 mmHg with retinal haemorrhage/papilloedema, or with life-threatening symptoms.
  • Start treatment: all persistent stage 2; stage 1 when risk is elevated (target organ damage/CVD/renal disease/diabetes/10-year risk >=10%).
  • Targets: clinic BP <140/90 (<80 years) and <150/90 (>=80 years); home targets 5 mmHg lower.
  • Primary care follow-up: titrate every 4–6 weeks until controlled, then annual review.
